Quick and Easy Instant Spice Mix Rendang Beef Recipe

Using instant rendang spice mix and a few additional ingredients, this recipe offers a flavorful, simplified approach to rendang beef. With a cooking time of around 2 hours, it's perfect for those who want a delicious homemade rendang without spending an entire day in the kitchen. Ideal for anyone looking to clear out some freezer space and enjoy a comforting, spiced meal with ease.

Nothing beats the aroma of slow-cooked rendang filling the kitchen. After seeing that beef sitting in the freezer, I decided it was time to put it to good use. With a bit of instant rendang spice mix and some extra ingredients, I whipped up a deliciously rich rendang without the usual hassle. This recipe is perfect for a relaxed cooking day at home!

2 Hours
Serves 3-4

Main Ingredients:

  • 500 g beef, thinly sliced
  • Water
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 pack of instant coconut milk (like Kara)
  • 1 packet of rendang instant spice mix (such as Bamboe)
  • 2 tbsp sweet soy sauce
  • Sugar and MSG to taste

Spice Paste:

  • 10 shallots
  • 7 cloves garlic
  • Use fresh beef for the best flavor and texture.
  • Adjust the amount of water to control the thickness of the sauce.
  • Add the kaffir lime leaves and bay leaf for an authentic aroma.
  • Cook on low heat for a longer time to ensure the beef becomes tender.
  • Stir regularly to prevent the sauce from sticking to the pan.
  • If you prefer a creamier texture, add more coconut milk.
  • Sweet soy sauce adds a hint of sweetness; adjust according to preference.
  • Balance MSG and sugar carefully to enhance flavor without overpowering it.

1. Prepare the Beef

Slice the beef thinly, rinse, and place it in a pot of boiling water. Add kaffir lime leaves and bay leaf to the water for extra flavor.

2. Boil the Beef

Let the beef simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until tender. Drain the beef but save the broth for later.

3. Sauté the Spices

Grind or blend the shallots and garlic into a paste. Sauté this mixture until fragrant, then add the instant spice mix. Be careful not to let it burn, as it could give the rendang a bitter taste.

4. Combine and Cook

Add the beef to the pan and mix well with the spices. Pour in the reserved broth and the coconut milk.

5. Season

Add sugar, MSG, and sweet soy sauce to taste. Stir, cover, and let simmer until the sauce has thickened and absorbed.

6. Adjust and Serve

Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve hot with rice, and enjoy your simplified rendang!
